PolicyMap Decision Tree: Evaluating Violent Content Across Four Platforms

A structured decision framework for Trust & Safety professionals reviewing real-world violent content.
Every node is YES/NO only. Every terminal node states an action. Colour coding: 🔴 Remove · 🟠 Restrict · ⬛ Human Review · 🔵 Platform-specific action · 🟢 Document & close.

📋 Scenario: Real-world violent video submitted for review 📅 Policy sources: May 2025 🔍 Primary sources only — no secondary summaries 📄 Companion: PolicyMap_Report_v1.0.docx | PolicyMap_Audit_Matrix.xlsx
REMOVE — content violates policy, removal required on all platforms
RESTRICT — age-gate, warning screen, or FYF restriction applied
HUMAN REVIEW — automated decision insufficient, escalate
PLATFORM-SPECIFIC — outcome differs by platform, see node
DOCUMENT — decision made, log rationale and close
START: A video contains real-world violence or graphic content. What do you do?
Begin at Node 1 and follow YES/NO paths. Every path leads to a documented action.
Node 1
Does the content depict minors being harmed, sexually exploited, or killed?
Includes imagery, audio, or text describing harm to any person under 18.
YES →
🚫 IMMEDIATE REMOVAL — ALL PLATFORMS
Report to NCMEC (legal obligation for all 4 platforms). Contact law enforcement if credible imminent threat. No EDSA/context exception applies to CSAM on any platform. End.
Policy anchors: YouTube answer/2801999 · Reddit HC articles/360008810092 · TikTok community-guidelines/en/safety-civility · Meta CS/child-sexual-exploitation-abuse-nudity
NO → Continue to Node 2
Node 2
Does the content glorify, celebrate, instruct, or incite the viewer to commit violence?
Does it praise perpetrators, share their manifesto, or frame violence as heroic or desirable?
YES →
🚫 REMOVAL — ALL PLATFORMS
No contextual exceptions apply to content that glorifies or incites violence. End.

YT: "content encouraging others to commit violent acts" — answer/2802008
Reddit: "encourages, glorifies, incites, or calls for violence" — HCA/360043513151
TikTok: "glorifying violence" — CG/safety-civility
Meta: "celebrates the suffering or humiliation of others" — CS/violent-graphic-content
NO → Continue to Node 3
Node 3
Was this footage filmed by the perpetrator of the violent event?
"Perpetrator footage" = filmed by the person committing the violence, not bystanders or journalists.
YES →
🚫 REMOVAL — YOUTUBE (absolute rule)
⬛ LIKELY REMOVAL — TikTok & Meta
🟠 SUBREDDIT-DEPENDENT — Reddit
YouTube: "Footage filmed by the perpetrator during a deadly or major violent event...is NOT allowed even with EDSA context" — answer/6345162. This is YouTube's hardest rule. End.
NO → Continue to Node 4
Node 4
Is journalistic, educational, or documentary context present IN the video itself — not only in the title or description?
Context = narration, text overlay, on-screen captions explaining who/what/when/why. Description-only context DOES NOT qualify on YouTube (highest-risk categories).
NO — no in-video context →
⬛ PLATFORM-SPECIFIC — No in-video context
🔴 YouTube: REMOVE or AGE-GATE (without in-video context, EDSA exception unlikely for high-risk categories)

🔴 TikTok: LIKELY REMOVE (no news exception documented without in-video public-interest framing)

🟠 Reddit: NSFW label option IF subreddit rules allow; else REMOVE

🟠 Meta: REMOVE or WARNING SCREEN — graphic content requires context to be shared to "raise awareness"

Action: Request creator re-post with in-video narration or contextual overlay. End.
YES — context is in the video →
Node 5
Is the violence depicted gratuitously — does it dwell on, linger on, or celebrate suffering beyond what the editorial purpose requires?
"Gratuitous" = slow-motion replays of death, zooming in on injuries, reactions that frame violence approvingly. Not the same as graphic — context is about framing, not pixel content.
YES — gratuitous →
🚫 REMOVAL — ALL PLATFORMS
EDSA exceptions require that suffering not be presented gratuitously. Content meant to "shock or disgust" is not saved by having a news label.

YouTube: "intent to shock or disgust viewers" — answer/2802008
Meta: "glorifies violence or celebrates the suffering or humiliation of others" — CS/violent-graphic-content. End.
NO — editorial framing present →
Node 6
Which platform are you moderating?
Platform-specific rules now apply. Content has passed all universal filters. See platform cards below.
🎬 YouTube
Apply EDSA Evaluation:
1. Is context in the video/audio? (confirmed YES at Node 4)
2. Is content from an active warzone, humanitarian crisis, or major civil event?
→ If YES: AGE-GATE (not viewable under 18 or signed out)
→ May also add warning screen
→ If sufficient public interest: ALLOW with age restriction

Source: support.google.com/youtube/answer/2802008 — "We may allow or age-restrict graphic or violent content documenting warzones, civil unrest, humanitarian crises...with adequate educational, documentary, scientific or artistic context."
🎵 TikTok
Two-decision process — removal AND FYF:
1. Does content meet explicit news/public-interest value? → May be AGE-RESTRICTED (18+)
2. Even if not removed: evaluate for FYF ELIGIBILITY separately — borderline graphic content is FYF Ineligible even if kept on platform
3. If unclear: HUMAN REVIEW

Source: tiktok.com/community-guidelines/en/overview — "Graphic footage of events...in the public interest to view, such as wars and major disasters [may be] age-restricted or FYF-ineligible." Note: TikTok's threshold is stricter than YouTube — graphic content defaults to removal without clear news value.
🟠 Reddit
Check subreddit rules FIRST:
1. Does the specific subreddit allow graphic content?
2. Is NSFW label applied or required? → ALLOW WITH NSFW LABEL if subreddit permits
3. Does content have public interest value (news reporting, historical documentation, citizen journalism) AND not mock victims or promote perpetrator? → ALLOW WITH NSFW LABEL
4. No label applied and subreddit does not allow graphic content? → FLAG FOR MOD

Source: support.reddithelp.com HCA/360043513151 — "We may allow appropriately labelled graphic content that has public interest value, such as legitimate news reporting, historical documentation, or citizen journalism."
📘 Meta / Instagram
Apply Newsworthiness Evaluation:
1. Is content shared to condemn, raise awareness, or provide news coverage? → Likely ALLOW WITH RESTRICTIONS
2. Could users under 18 see it? → Apply AGE-GATE (hidden from under-18s)
3. Add WARNING SCREEN for users over 18 ("Graphic or violent content")
4. Political/public figure content? Consider formal NEWSWORTHINESS ALLOWANCE

Source: transparency.meta.com/policies/community-standards/violent-graphic-content — "Graphic content shared to raise awareness about current events may be allowed but hidden from users under 18 with a warning screen."
✅ END: Document your decision and rationale in the review log.
Record: platform, node path taken, policy anchors cited, action applied, timestamp. If escalated to human review, record reviewer ID and outcome.
⚠️ Critical Cross-Platform Gap Documented in This Decision Tree (GAP-003 from PolicyMap Audit)
Node 6 reveals a structural philosophical difference: On TikTok, the moderator must make two separate decisions — (1) should this be removed? and (2) should this be FYF-ineligible even if it stays up? On YouTube, Reddit, and Meta, the second question is not formally documented as a policy action. A moderator following YouTube, Reddit, or Meta policy has no documented framework for "allow but suppress algorithmically." On TikTok, the policy explicitly requires this two-step evaluation.

This gap means: the same video that passes all removal tests on TikTok may still be effectively invisible to 95% of the platform's users — a fact the moderator is expected to account for but which no other platform's policy acknowledges as a distinct enforcement state.
