Three Headline Findings

Every finding is sourced from primary platform policy documents. No secondary sources were used.

🔴 TikTok's FYF De-amplification Is a Structural Innovation With No Cross-Platform Equivalent

TikTok is the only platform that explicitly documents a mid-tier enforcement action between "removal" and "allow." Content can exist on the platform and be effectively invisible to most users. The question for moderators is no longer just "should this be removed?" — it's also "should this be algorithmically suppressed even if it stays up?" No other platform has a documented answer to that second question.

🟡 Reddit Has a Documented Site-Wide Policy Absence on Misinformation

Reddit's Content Policy has no standalone misinformation policy. False health claims and election falsehoods are not explicitly prohibited at the platform level — confirmed by Reddit's own regulatory compliance filings under New York S895B (2025). This is unique among major platforms and has no equivalent on YouTube, TikTok, or Meta.

🔵 Meta's January 2025 Changes Introduced Documented LGBTQ+ Protection Rollbacks

Meta's updated Hateful Conduct policy now explicitly permits content alleging mental illness or abnormality based on gender or sexual orientation. This is documented in Meta's own January 2025 Change Log — not a rumour or third-party claim. Content that may be removed on TikTok or YouTube may now be explicitly permitted under Meta's current policy.

23 Documented Policy Gaps

Each gap is documented with verbatim policy quotes, a real-world scenario, harm potential, and a harmonisation recommendation. Full detail in the Gap Register spreadsheet.

GAP-001 · HIGH

Educational Violence Threshold

Same war footage: YouTube age-gates with narration present. TikTok removes regardless of context.

GAP-002 · HIGH

Slur Reclamation Standard

Meta explicitly allows self-referential slur use. TikTok has no documented exception for reclaimed language.

GAP-003 · HIGH

FYF De-amplification vs. Removal

TikTok's mid-tier enforcement (de-amplify without removal) has no equivalent documented on any other platform.

GAP-004 · HIGH

Misinformation Vacuum on Reddit

Reddit's site-wide Content Policy has no standalone misinformation policy. Confirmed by Reddit's own regulatory filings.

GAP-005 · HIGH

Oversight Mechanism Gap

Meta has an external Oversight Board with binding authority. YouTube, TikTok, Reddit: platform is final decision-maker.

GAP-006 · HIGH

Newsworthiness Transparency

Meta publishes data on exception invocations (32 in 1 year). YouTube, TikTok, Reddit: zero disclosure.

GAP-007 · HIGH

Regional Content Modulation

TikTok explicitly documents regional suppression with no creator notification. No other platform documents this.

GAP-008 · HIGH

Safe Messaging Guidelines

YouTube and Meta explicitly reference clinical Safe Messaging Guidelines. TikTok and Reddit do not name them in primary policy.

GAP-009 · MEDIUM

Strike System Differences

YouTube: formal 3-strike system. Reddit: no site-wide strikes. TikTok: tier-based warnings. Meta: not documented publicly.

GAP-010 · MEDIUM

Public Figure Exception Thresholds

Different tests for political satire across platforms produce different outcomes for borderline content.

GAP-011 · MEDIUM

Animal Cruelty Exceptions

YouTube enumerates specific exceptions (hunting, food). TikTok does not enumerate equivalent exceptions.

GAP-012 · MEDIUM

Coordinated Inauthentic Behaviour

Meta and TikTok have named CIB policies. Reddit's "content manipulation" rule is broader and less specific.

GAP-013 · MEDIUM

Glorification vs. Discussion

Four different operational tests for "glorification." Same moderator decision, four different policy anchors.

GAP-014 · MEDIUM

Dangerous Challenges Threshold

YouTube: "imminent risk of injury." TikTok: "could lead to harm." Legally and operationally significant threshold difference.

GAP-015 · MEDIUM

Off-Platform Behaviour

TikTok explicitly documents off-platform conduct review. YouTube and Reddit do not.

GAP-016 · MEDIUM

Satire Exception Documentation

Meta has an explicit named satire exception. TikTok has no equivalent named exception in its Community Guidelines.

GAP-017 · MEDIUM

Historical Documentation Exception

Reddit explicitly names historical documentation as an exception. TikTok does not.

GAP-018 · MEDIUM

Operator/Creator/User Liability Chain

YouTube documents a three-tier responsibility chain. TikTok and Meta do not document equivalent creator liability distinctions.

GAP-019 · LOW

Terminology Differences

Four different labels for the same hate speech category across four platforms.

GAP-020 · LOW

Appeal Timeline Documentation

No platform publishes specific per-case appeal timeline commitments.

GAP-021 · LOW

Community Warning Labels

Reddit's NSFW system gives users active consent control. TikTok's age-restriction is binary with no equivalent label.

GAP-022 · LOW

Context Burden Allocation

YouTube places context burden on creators. TikTok places it on moderators. Different allocation, different outcomes.

GAP-023 · LOW

Transparency Reporting Granularity

Meta: quarterly category-level data. Reddit: least granular of the four. Makes cross-platform research significantly harder.
