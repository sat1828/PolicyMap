## YOUTUBE — Live verified: support.google.com/youtube

### [YT-V-01] Violent Content — General Rule
> "Violent or gory content intended to shock or disgust viewers, or content encouraging others to commit violent acts, are not allowed on YouTube."

Source: support.google.com/youtube/answer/2802008

### [YT-V-02] News/Warzone Context Exception
> "We may allow or age-restrict graphic or violent content documenting warzones, civil unrest, humanitarian crises, or natural disasters with adequate educational, documentary, scientific or artistic context in the images or audio of the video, the video title, or the video description."

Source: support.google.com/youtube/answer/2802008

### [YT-V-03] Perpetrator Footage — Absolute Prohibition (No EDSA Exception)
> "Note that we may not allow the following kinds of content even if there's educational, documentary, scientific, or artistic context provided: ... Footage filmed by the perpetrator during a deadly or major violent event, in which weapons, violence, or injured victims are visible or audible."

Source: support.google.com/youtube/answer/2802008

### [YT-V-04] Dramatised Violence — Age-Restriction Rule
> "We may age-restrict dramatized violence when it contains graphic scenes, such as mass violence against non-combatants or people being tortured, dismembered, or decapitated. We make a distinction between dramatized violence featuring real human actors and content featuring animations or video games."

Source: support.google.com/youtube/answer/2802008

### [YT-V-05] Animal Abuse — Traditional Practice Exceptions
> "We may make exceptions for content that shows widely accepted practices, like hunting, trapping, pest abatement, food preparation, medical treatment, or animal slaughter that shows harm to an animal or group of animals."

Source: support.google.com/youtube/answer/2802008

### [YT-E-01] EDSA — Context Location Rule (THE KEY RULE)
> "Important: For content that risks the most harm, we require context in the VIDEO or AUDIO. The video and audio are the parts of the content where viewers will most likely get the context, including when the content is embedded on other websites or apps. Content where we require context in the audio or video to give an EDSA exception includes content related to hate speech, violent criminal organizations, child safety, suicide and self-harm, and graphic violence."

Source: support.google.com/youtube/answer/6345162

### [YT-E-02] EDSA — Public Interest Scope
> "In some cases, we may make EDSA exceptions to allow certain content based on public interest. This could include, for example, content like congressional or parliamentary proceedings, campaign speeches, debates over ongoing government actions, graphic footage from active warzones or humanitarian crises, or comments disputing health authority guidance made during a public hearing."

Source: support.google.com/youtube/answer/6345162

### [YT-H-01] Hate Speech — Protected Attributes
> "Hate speech is not allowed on YouTube. We don't allow content that promotes violence or hatred against individuals or groups based on any of the following attributes, which indicate a protected group status under YouTube's policy: Age / Caste, Ethnicity, or Race / Disability / Immigration Status / Nationality / Religion / Sex, Gender, or Sexual Orientation / Veteran Status / Victims of a major violent event and their kin"

Source: support.google.com/youtube/answer/2801939

### [YT-H-02] Hate Speech — EDSA Context Location
> "For educational, documentary, scientific, or artistic content that includes hate speech, this context must appear in the images or audio of the video itself. Providing it in the title or description is insufficient."

Source: support.google.com/youtube/answer/2801939

### [YT-H-03] Hate Speech — EDSA Exception Types
> "Additional context may include condemning, refuting, including opposing views, or satirizing hate speech."

Source: support.google.com/youtube/answer/2801939

### [YT-S-01] Self-Harm — Prohibited Content
> "we do not allow content on YouTube that promotes suicide, self-harm, or eating disorders, that is intended to shock or disgust, or that poses a considerable risk to viewers"

Source: support.google.com/youtube/answer/2802245

### [YT-S-02] Self-Harm — Method Instructions Prohibited
> "Instructions on how to die by suicide, engage in self-harm, or engage in eating disorders (including how to conceal them)" [prohibited even with EDSA context]

Source: support.google.com/youtube/answer/2802245

### [YT-D-01] Harassment & Doxxing
> "We don't allow content that targets someone with prolonged insults or slurs based on their physical traits or protected group status. We also don't allow other harmful behaviors, like threats or doxxing."

Source: support.google.com/youtube/answer/2802268

### [YT-D-02] PII / Doxxing Definition
> "Content that shares, threatens to share, or encourages others to share non-public personally identifiable information (PII). PII includes, but isn't limited to, home addresses; email addresses; sign-in credentials, like a username or password; phone numbers; passport numbers; medical records; bank account information; or the name of a school a minor attends."

Source: support.google.com/youtube/answer/2802268

### [YT-C-01] Dangerous Challenges — Threshold
> "Extremely dangerous challenges: Challenges that pose an imminent risk of physical injury."

Source: support.google.com/youtube/answer/2801964

### [YT-ST-01] Strikes System
> "If this is your first time violating our Community Guidelines, you'll likely get a warning with no penalty to your channel. You will have the option to take a policy training to allow the warning to expire after 90 days. The 90 day period starts from when the training is completed, not when the warning is issued... If you get 3 strikes within 90 days, your channel may be terminated."

Source: support.google.com/youtube/answer/2802008

## REDDIT — Live verified: support.reddithelp.com

### [RD-V-01] Violence — Public Interest Exception
> "Additionally, we may allow appropriately labelled graphic content that has public interest value, such as legitimate news reporting, historical documentation, or citizen journalism, provided it is not used to mock victims, celebrate violence, or promote a perpetrator's message or ideology."

Source: support.reddithelp.com/hc/en-us/articles/360043513151

### [RD-V-02] Violence — NSFW Label Requirement
> "Note: Content that depicts violence but doesn't violate this policy (as described above) should be labeled Not Safe for Work ('NSFW')."

Source: support.reddithelp.com/hc/en-us/articles/360043513151

### [RD-V-03] Violence — Self-Harm Instructions Prohibited
> "A post that provides instructions on methods of self-harm." [listed as prohibited example]

Source: support.reddithelp.com/hc/en-us/articles/360043513151

### [RD-H-01] Hate — Context-Based Evaluation
> "Context is critical. When evaluating a report, we assess the activity of a community or an individual user, their pattern of behavior, and the content itself. This allows us to distinguish genuine hateful attacks, bad faith 'humor,' or coded language from content shared for purposes like social or political commentary, educational uses, or condemnation of hate."

Source: support.reddithelp.com/hc/en-us/articles/360045715951

### [RD-P-01] Harassment — Public Figure Exception
> "Note: Public figures may be subject to robust criticism and scrutiny related to their public role or persona, but this does not permit harassment campaigns that directly target their family, compromise their personal safety, or incite serious harm upon them."

Source: support.reddithelp.com/hc/en-us/articles/360043071072

### [RD-EN-01] Enforcement — Automated + LLM
> "Our safety teams use a combination of automated tooling and human review, and are constantly building and refining tools to proactively identify content and behavior that violates our policies at scale. Reddit also uses large language models (LLMs) trained in-house to automatically review certain categories of content that have been flagged for review."

Source: support.reddithelp.com/hc/en-us/articles/23511059871252

### [RD-MI-01] Misinformation — ABSENT (Policy Gap)
> Reddit's Content Policy as of May 2025 contains no standalone rule prohibiting false factual claims about health or elections. Rules 2 (content manipulation) and 5 (impersonation) are the closest provisions — neither directly prohibits health or election misinformation.

Confirmed: redditinc.com/policies/content-policy; NY S895B Reddit compliance filing (ag.ny.gov)

## TIKTOK — Live verified: tiktok.com/community-guidelines

### [TK-F-01] FYF — Core De-amplification Rule
> "When we identify content that falls under the 'FYF Ineligible' standards in our Community Guidelines, it won't be recommended in the FYF."

Source: tiktok.com/community-guidelines/en/overview (confirmed live, May 2025)

### [TK-F-02] FYF — Standards Description
> "We maintain content eligibility standards for the FYF that prioritize safety, and are informed by the diversity of our community and cultural practices. While the spontaneity of the FYF is what makes TikTok unique, it is intended for a range of audiences that includes everyone from teenagers to great grandparents. We make ineligible for the FYF, and may also make harder to find in search, certain content that may not be suitable for a broad audience."

Source: tiktok.com/community-guidelines/en/fyf-standards (confirmed live, May 2025)

### [TK-F-03] FYF — Creator Analytics Notification
> "Creators can check TikTok's analytics to see how their videos are performing, including if there were any made ineligible for recommendation."

Source: tiktok.com/community-guidelines/en/fyf-standards

### [TK-H-01] Hate Speech — General Rule
> "Hate Speech and Hateful Behavior: We don't allow content that promotes hate or attacks people based on protected attributes like race, religion, gender, or sexual orientation."

Source: tiktok.com/community-guidelines/en/overview (confirmed live, May 2025)

### [TK-H-02] Hate Speech — FYF for Indirect Demeaning
> "Some content that uses stereotypes, insinuation, or indirect statements that may implicitly demean protected groups [is FYF ineligible]"

Source: tiktok.com/community-guidelines/en/fyf-standards (confirmed live, May 2025)

### [TK-V-01] Violence — General Rule
> "Violent and Criminal Behavior: We don't allow threats, encouragement or glorification of violence, promotion of crime, or instructions on how to commit harmful acts."

Source: tiktok.com/community-guidelines/en/overview (confirmed live, May 2025)

### [TK-ST-01] Strikes — Warning Strike System (April 2024 Update)
> "TikTok is introducing a 'warning strike' when a user violates the community guidelines for the first time. The warning strike does not count toward an account's strike tally, but any future violations will. Accounts that violate TikTok's 'zero-tolerance' policies (such as incitement to violence) aren't eligible for such reminders and will immediately be banned."

Source: TikTok April 2024 Community Guidelines announcement (newsroom.tiktok.com); confirmed via Variety reporting

## META / INSTAGRAM — Live verified: transparency.meta.com

### [MT-HC-01] Hateful Conduct — Protected Characteristics Definition
> "We define hateful conduct as direct attacks against people — rather than concepts or institutions — on the basis of what we call protected characteristics (PCs): race, ethnicity, national origin, disability, religious affiliation, caste, sexual orientation, sex, gender identity, and serious disease."

Source: transparency.meta.com/policies/community-standards/hateful-conduct/ (confirmed live, May 2025)

### [MT-HC-02] LGBTQ+ Mental Illness Exception — January 2025 Change (CRITICAL)
> "We do allow allegations of mental illness or abnormality when based on gender or sexual orientation, given political and religious discourse about transgenderism and homosexuality and common non-serious usage of words like 'weird.'"

Source: transparency.meta.com/policies/community-standards/hateful-conduct/ (live confirmed, May 2025)
Corroborated by: GLAAD 2025 SMSI, GLAAD 2026 SMSI, NBC News (Jan 7 2025), PolitiFact/Poynter (Jan 14 2025)
Status as of May 2025: STILL IN EFFECT per GLAAD 2026 SMSI (published ~May 2025)

### [MT-HC-03] Social Exclusion — New Jan 2025 Provision
> "Social exclusion, which means things like denying access to spaces (physical and online) and social services, except for sex or gender-based exclusion from spaces commonly limited by sex or gender, such as restrooms, sports and sports leagues, health and support groups, and specific schools."

Source: transparency.meta.com/policies/community-standards/hateful-conduct/ (confirmed live, May 2025)

### [MT-HC-04] Slur Reclamation Exception
> "We recognize that people sometimes share content that includes slurs or someone else's speech in order to condemn the speech or report on it. In other cases, speech, including slurs, that might otherwise violate our standards is used self-referentially or in an empowering way. We allow this type of speech where the speaker's intention is clear."

Source: transparency.meta.com/policies/community-standards/hateful-conduct/ (confirmed live, May 2025)

### [MT-VG-01] Violent/Graphic — Newsworthiness + Age-Gate
> "Graphic content shared to raise awareness about current events may be allowed but hidden from users under 18 with a warning screen for those over 18. We remove content that glorifies violence or celebrates the suffering or humiliation of others."

Source: transparency.meta.com/policies/community-standards/violent-graphic-content/

### [MT-S-01] Satire Exception
> "In certain cases, we will allow content that may otherwise violate the Community Standards when it is determined that the content is satirical. Content will only be allowed if the violating elements of the content are being satirized or attributed to something or someone else in order to mock or criticize them."

Source: transparency.meta.com/policies/community-standards/

### [MT-MI-01] Misinformation — Acknowledged Limitation
> "Misinformation is different from other types of speech addressed in our Community Standards because there is no way to articulate a comprehensive list of what is prohibited...The world is changing constantly, and what is true one minute may not be true the next minute."

Source: transparency.meta.com/policies/community-standards/misinformation/

### [MT-OB-01] Oversight Board — April 2025 LGBTQ+ Ruling
> Meta's Oversight Board in April 2025 ruled on two videos featuring intentional misgendering and rebuked Meta's January 2025 Hateful Conduct changes. As of May 2025, Meta has not reversed the January 2025 changes.

Source: GLAAD 2026 SMSI (glaad.org/smsi/2026/meta-platforms/, published ~May 2025)
